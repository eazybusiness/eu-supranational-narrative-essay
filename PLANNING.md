## Proposed Essay Structure

### Section 1 — The Institutional Appropriation of "Europe"
- EEC as default meaning of "Europe" (Patel thesis)
- Hallstein's discursive strategy (Commission speeches, Florence archives)
- Symbolic competition with Council of Europe and OEEC
- How the institutions positioned themselves as Europe's legitimate voice

### Section 2 — Peace, Memory, and Sacred Legitimacy
- The peace narrative as primary myth (Monnet, Schuman, De Gasperi)
- Kohl's 1994 framing: Europe = war or peace
- Risse's constitutive argument: the EU produces European identity, not just reflects it
- Decline of living memory and the fragility of trauma-based legitimacy

### Section 3 — The Constitutional Narrative of the Court of Justice
- Van Gend en Loos (1963): "new legal order" — narrative as much as legal judgment
- Costa v. ENEL (1964): supremacy as foundational story
- Simmenthal (1978): consolidating the constitutional edifice
- ECJ as narrator of a sui generis polity
- Speeches by Pilotti and subsequent Court presidents

### Section 4 — Manufactured Identity: Symbols, Rituals, and the People's Europe
- Adonnino Committee (1985): flag, anthem, passport, Europe Day
- European Parliament's first direct elections (1979) as legitimation event
- Propaganda campaigns: "Europe in 10 Lessons", "Building Europe Together"
- The paradox: needing to manufacture what nation-states inherited organically

### Section 5 — The Limits of Supranational Myth-Making
- Della Sala critique: EU myths lack emotional resonance vs. national myths
- Absence of a narrative arc (struggle → triumph) in the EU story
- The democratic legitimacy crisis (Maastricht ratification, Brexit)
- Post-enlargement identity fragmentation
- Competing nationalisms as counter-narrative
